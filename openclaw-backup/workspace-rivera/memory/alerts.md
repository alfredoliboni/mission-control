## Active Alerts

### 1) Transition Planning Meeting with School
- Priority: High
- Target Window: Next school term planning cycle
- Why it matters: Transition goals should be built into the IEP before high school years advance too far.
- Action: Request school meeting focused specifically on transition, employment readiness, independence, and post-secondary pathway options.
- Notes: Ask who is responsible for transition planning at school and how employment goals will be tracked.

### 2) Vocational Assessment Scheduling
- Priority: High
- Target Window: Within 3 to 6 months
- Why it matters: A formal vocational or functional transition assessment can guide realistic job matches, accommodations, and training needs.
- Action: Identify assessment options through school, private providers, or community agencies.
- Notes: Request attention to ASD + ADHD, sensory profile, and communication style.

### 3) ODSP Pre-Application Research
- Priority: Medium to High
- Target Window: Start before age 17 if possible
- Why it matters: Documentation gathering is easier when started early. The family should understand medical, functional, financial, and timeline requirements well before age 18.
- Action: Build a checklist for ODSP transition prep, including updated assessments and physician/psychologist documentation.
- Notes: Clarify interaction with DTC, RDSP, and future employment earnings.

### 4) Summer Employment Program Deadlines
- Priority: Seasonal / Medium
- Target Window: Watch each winter and spring for upcoming summer intake deadlines
- Why it matters: Youth employment and readiness programs often have limited spaces and early intake periods.
- Action: Check local boards, Employment Ontario providers, city/community programs, and disability-inclusive employment initiatives.
- Notes: Prioritize lower-pressure placements with coaching or structured supervision.
