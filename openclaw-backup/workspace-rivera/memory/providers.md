## Mississauga / Peel Provider Map
This file is a practical provider map for transition-to-employment planning. It mixes verified system-level providers with a short list of provider types still needing direct outreach.

## Highest-Priority Local Providers

### 1. ErinoakKids
- Region: Peel, including Mississauga
- Type: Children’s treatment centre
- Relevant services: developmental disability supports, autism-related services, therapy, service navigation, family-centred planning
- Why it matters for Mateus: strong fit for coordinating transition-related supports while he is still a youth, especially if the family needs help connecting therapy goals to independence, regulation, and community participation.
- Best use: ask whether there are transition-planning, adolescent OT, social work, or service-navigation routes that can support employment-readiness planning.
- Source: https://www.erinoakkids.ca/

### 2. Peel District School Board, special education + transition pathway
- Region: Mississauga / Peel
- Type: Public school board system support
- Relevant services: IEP-based transition planning, experiential learning, Student Success, guidance, co-op, board-linked special education supports
- Why it matters for Mateus: this is likely the most immediate route to structured transition planning, school documentation, and early work exposure.
- Best use: ask the school for a transition-planning meeting and request links to guidance, Student Success, experiential learning, and any board OYAP/co-op contacts.
- Verification note: exact page path was not fetchable during this session, so board-specific web citation should be rechecked before external sharing.
- Source to verify: https://www.pdsb.net/
- Source: Ontario special education transition planning guidance, https://www.ontario.ca/page/special-education-ontario-policy-and-resource-guide-kindergarten-grade-12

### 3. Dufferin-Peel Catholic District School Board, Special Education
- Region: Mississauga / Peel
- Type: Catholic school board system support
- Relevant services: school-based special education planning, consultant referrals, support services, inclusive student-centred planning
- Why it matters for Mateus: relevant if he attends a Catholic board school, especially for transition planning and school-based referrals.
- Best use: confirm school board enrollment first, then use board special education and guidance routes to push transition/employment planning.
- Source: https://www.dpcdsb.org/programs-services/special-education

### 4. Employment Ontario service network
- Region: Ontario, with local offices serving Mississauga / Peel
- Type: Government-funded employment service network
- Relevant services: employment counselling, resume help, interview preparation, skills training connections, youth job supports, apprenticeship information
- Why it matters for Mateus: one of the best later-teen entry points for local employment coaches, job developers, and vocational referrals.
- Best use: identify nearby offices and ask directly which sites have experience with autistic youth, youth with disabilities, supported employment, or vocational assessment referrals.
- Source: https://www.ontario.ca/page/employment-ontario

### 5. Ready, Willing and Able (RWA)
- Region: Canada-wide
- Type: Inclusive employment initiative
- Relevant services: employer engagement and employment pathways for autistic people and people with intellectual disabilities
- Why it matters for Mateus: more useful in later teen years when direct employer matching becomes realistic, but worth tracking now because it aligns closely with autism employment outcomes.
- Best use: keep on the future-employment list rather than as the first immediate next step.
- Source: https://readywillingable.ca/

## Provider Categories To Contact Next

### Vocational rehabilitation / employment coaching
Look for agencies that can provide one or more of:
- vocational assessment
- work-readiness coaching
- supported employment or job carving
- travel training
- workplace communication coaching
- employer matching for youth with disabilities

Recommended search route:
1. school guidance / Student Success / special education
2. local Employment Ontario offices
3. autism or disability employment agencies
4. community agencies serving youth with developmental disabilities

Recommendation: start with school and Employment Ontario before paying privately, because they may identify no-cost or subsidized local routes.
- Source: https://www.ontario.ca/page/employment-ontario
- Source: https://www.ontario.ca/page/special-education-ontario-policy-and-resource-guide-kindergarten-grade-12

### ASD + ADHD therapy providers for adolescent transition goals
Priority disciplines:
- occupational therapy for sensory needs, executive functioning, routines, and independence
- psychology or psychotherapy for self-advocacy, emotional regulation, anxiety, and transition stress
- speech-language therapy if workplace communication or conversational stamina becomes a barrier

What to ask before booking:
- Do they work with autistic teens who also have ADHD?
- Do they build practical goals around school transition, employment readiness, or community independence?
- Do they collaborate with schools or accept outside reports?
- Can they support short, concrete communication styles rather than insight-heavy talk therapy only?
- Do they offer parent coaching for carryover at home?

## Best-Fit Provider Features for Mateus
Prefer providers who:
- work comfortably with concrete, stepwise goals
- understand both autism and ADHD, not one or the other only
- can translate therapy goals into school, work, and daily-living routines
- are open to sensory accommodations
- can document functional impact clearly for future benefits or transition planning
