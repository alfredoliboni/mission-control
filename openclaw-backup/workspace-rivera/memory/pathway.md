# Mateus Rivera - Pathway

Last Updated: 2026-04-07
Status: Active

## Current Stage
**Pre-Employment**

Mateus is in the early transition-to-work phase. The goal right now is not immediate paid employment, but building a foundation through assessment, transition planning, skills practice, and low-pressure work exposure.

## Journey Overview
### Completed / In Progress Foundations
- [x] Autism diagnosis established
- [x] ADHD comorbidity identified
- [x] School support in place through IEP
- [x] ABA therapy in place, now reducing
- [x] Family has identified employment and independence as major priorities
- [x] Interest area identified: computers / technology
- [ ] Transition plan formally documented and reviewed with school team
- [ ] Vocational assessment completed
- [ ] Job shadowing experience arranged
- [ ] Employment-related skills training started in a structured way

## Current Stage Goals
### Pre-Employment Milestones
- [ ] Vocational assessment
  - Purpose: identify strengths, support needs, sensory considerations, work pace, and accommodation profile
- [ ] Job shadowing
  - Priority settings: computer-related, structured office tasks, library/tech support, stock/inventory, or kitchen prep with sensory screening
- [ ] Skills training
  - Focus: self-advocacy, transportation, workplace communication, time management, task completion, emotional regulation on the job
- [ ] Transition plan development
  - Include school team, family, and where possible Mateus's own voice and preferences

## Suggested Stage Sequence
### Now to Next 6 Months
1. Confirm who is leading transition planning at school
2. Request IEP review with explicit transition and employment goals
3. Book vocational assessment
4. Start one practical community exposure activity
5. Build a document folder for transition records

### Next 6 to 18 Months
1. Add regular volunteer or job-shadow experience
2. Track accommodations that help in real settings
3. Introduce travel training or supported transportation routines
4. Explore summer youth employment or readiness programs
5. Revisit benefit planning as age 16 to 18 approaches

### Later Teen Years
1. School-to-community transition handoff
2. Employment service connection
3. Supported or customized employment exploration
4. Adult disability service planning
5. ODSP application timing and documentation readiness

## What Success Looks Like in This Stage
- Mateus can describe at least a few job interests and dislikes
- Family has a clearer sense of realistic job matches
- School transition plan includes measurable post-school goals
- One or more community experiences have been tried
- Accommodation needs are documented early, not guessed later

## Risks / Barriers to Watch
- Transition planning staying too general and not linked to actual employment steps
- Delaying vocational assessment until late high school
- ADHD-related executive functioning needs being mistaken for lack of motivation
- Sensory issues limiting job fit if not discussed early
- Family receiving fragmented advice from school, therapy, and adult systems
