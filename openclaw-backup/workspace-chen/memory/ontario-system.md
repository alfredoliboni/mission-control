# Emma Chen - Ontario System Reference

Last Updated: 2026-04-07
Status: Reference

## Purpose
Quick Ontario-facing reference for the systems most relevant to Emma’s transition from secondary school into adulthood.

## Main Systems To Track

### 1. TDSB / Secondary School Support System
Relevant areas:
- IEP and accommodation planning
- IPRC records where applicable
- course pathway and graduation planning
- guidance counseling
- co-op / experiential learning
- transition planning for post-secondary or work

Emma-specific relevance:
- Needs transition planning embedded into secondary school years, not deferred to the end.
- School should help document accommodations that work in academic and exam settings.

Source: USER.md and Ontario school support norms

### 2. Ontario Post-Secondary Accessibility System
Relevant areas:
- university and college accessibility offices
- disability documentation requirements
- academic accommodation approval
- exam supports and learning strategy supports

Emma-specific relevance:
- High because family is already thinking about university.
- Emma will likely need function-based accommodation language, not just diagnosis labels.

### 3. Ontario Adult Disability / Developmental Services System
Relevant areas:
- Developmental Services Ontario intake/navigation where relevant
- Passport-linked adult developmental supports where eligible
- transition-age planning to avoid service gaps

Emma-specific relevance:
- Should be reviewed early enough to understand whether adult developmental services fit her profile and needs.
- Eligibility cannot be assumed from diagnosis label alone.

### 4. ODSP System
Relevant areas:
- income support
- health-related benefits connected to eligibility
- Employment Supports stream

Emma-specific relevance:
- May become relevant depending on Emma’s adult schooling, work, and financial situation.
- Employment Supports may be especially worth tracking for a bright autistic young adult pursuing work.

### 5. Federal Disability Planning Layer
Relevant areas:
- DTC
- RDSP
- disability-related financial planning

Emma-specific relevance:
- Important because longer-term financial planning should happen before transition stress peaks.

## Transition-Age System Logic
### Before Leaving School
- Confirm what supports are school-based only.
- Build evidence of accommodations that help.
- Gather documentation.
- Start adult-system research before urgent need.

### During School Exit Planning
- Decide primary next step: university, college, gap year, work, supported employment, or mixed pathway.
- Connect accommodations and benefits planning to that decision.

### After School Exit
- Family often loses the convenience of school-centered coordination.
- Adult systems expect more self-advocacy, application management, and documentation readiness.

## Emma-Specific Practical Priorities
1. TDSB secondary supports and transition planning
2. Post-secondary accessibility processes
3. Co-op / experiential learning pathways
4. Adult service eligibility research
5. Financial/benefit transition readiness

## Questions To Answer Over Time
- Which post-secondary environments are most autism-friendly for Emma’s profile?
- Does Emma need updated formal testing?
- Which adult systems are actually likely to serve her well?
- What can be handled through school supports versus outside systems?

## Caution Notes
- Adult systems are often slower and less coordinated than school supports.
- Documentation quality matters.
- Functional needs should be described concretely: sensory environment, group work, communication nuance, executive functioning, and transition stress.
