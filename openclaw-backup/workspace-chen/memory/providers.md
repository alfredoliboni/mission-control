## Priority Provider Categories for Adolescent Transition

### 1. Therapists / Clinicians Specializing in Adolescent ASD
Use for:
- social communication and pragmatics
- anxiety/self-advocacy if needed
- executive functioning
- transition planning support
- updated documentation

Ideal fit for Emma:
- comfortable working with verbally fluent autistic teens
- strength-based approach
- understands masking, burnout, peer stress, and post-secondary transition
- able to translate support needs into functional accommodation language

### 2. Teen Social Skills Groups
Use for:
- peer relationships
- reading sarcasm and subtext
- managing group work
- friendship maintenance
- community confidence

Ideal fit for Emma:
- older teen group, not child-oriented
- intellectually appropriate peers
- avoids overly juvenile curriculum
- may include role-play for school, dating boundaries, group projects, and work settings

### 3. Vocational Counselors / Supported Employment Providers
Use for:
- vocational assessment
- strengths-based career exploration
- disclosure and accommodation planning
- resume building
- interview practice
- work placement matching

Ideal fit for Emma:
- understands autistic youth with strong academics
- can support tech-aligned opportunities
- experience with transition-age youth in Toronto

### 4. Post-Secondary Accessibility Contacts
Use for:
- disability documentation review
- academic accommodations
- note-taking, reduced distraction, exam supports
- assistive technology referrals
- transition-to-campus coaching

Ideal fit for Emma:
- institutions with solid STEM support culture
- clear processes and responsive accessibility teams
