## Priority Benefits

### 1. Disability Tax Credit (DTC)
- Priority: Urgent
- Why:
  - Often opens the door to other federal financial supports
  - Worth reviewing early after diagnosis, even if approval is not guaranteed
- Usual needs:
  - Medical certification from qualified practitioner
  - Functional impact documented clearly, not just diagnosis label
- Action:
  - Review form requirements and identify the best clinician to complete certification
- Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/segments/tax-credits-deductions-persons-disabilities/disability-tax-credit.html

### 2. Child Disability Benefit (CDB)
- Priority: High, dependent on DTC approval
- Why:
  - Provides monthly federal support for eligible families caring for a child eligible for the DTC
- Action:
  - If DTC is approved, confirm CDB flow and CRA account status
- Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/child-family-benefits/child-disability-benefit.html

### 3. Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP)
- Priority: High, after DTC approval
- Why:
  - Important long-term planning tool once DTC eligibility is in place
- Action:
  - Review setup options after DTC decision
- Note:
  - Long-term benefit, not the first urgent cash-flow step

### 4. Ontario Autism Program Timeline
- Priority: High
- Why:
  - OAP registration should happen immediately after diagnosis
  - Foundational supports may be available while waiting for other service streams
- Action:
  - Register through AccessOAP and track dates carefully
- Source: https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontario-autism-program

### 5. Emergency / Short-Term Respite
