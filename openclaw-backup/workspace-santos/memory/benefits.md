## Benefit Notes

### Ontario Autism Program funding
- Alex is clearly within OAP age and diagnosis eligibility.
- Important correction: official OAP "childhood budgets" are for children who registered before 2021-04. Alex registered in 2025-02, so the family’s phrase "waiting for Childhood Budget" likely needs clarification with AccessOAP.
- Practical interpretation: Alex should still be tracked as an active OAP family waiting for the next usable service/funding step, but not assumed to be on the legacy childhood budget stream.
- Family risk: high anxiety around missing OAP deadlines, so all OAP messages should be treated as urgent.

### Disability Tax Credit (DTC)
- Strong candidate to pursue.
- Usually the key unlock for federal disability benefits.
- If approved, DTC can also unlock:
  - Child Disability Benefit
  - RDSP grants and bonds
  - possible retroactive tax adjustments if CRA approves prior years
- Most important document: Form T2201 completed by a qualified medical practitioner.
- Family-specific note: communication, adaptive functioning, and prolonged daily support needs should be described concretely, not just diagnosis label.

### Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP)
- Not immediate cash flow, but high long-term value.
- Requires DTC approval first.
- Federal support can include:
  - Canada Disability Savings Grant, up to $3,500 per year depending on family income and contributions
  - Canada Disability Savings Bond, up to $1,000 per year for lower-income families even without contributions
- Good move after DTC approval, even if family can only contribute small amounts.

### Child Disability Benefit (CDB)
- Tax-free monthly payment for families caring for a child under 18 who is eligible for the DTC.
- For 2025-07 to 2026-06, official maximum is $3,411 per year, or $284.25 per month, per eligible child.
- Usually starts automatically if DTC is approved and the family already receives the Canada Child Benefit.

### Special Services at Home (SSAH)
- One of the most useful near-term Ontario programs for this family.
- Can reimburse or fund supports that fill gaps outside therapy waitlists.
- Good fits for Alex:
  - respite worker support
  - social skills / community participation costs
  - sensory supports
  - camps or structured activities
  - caregiver training/support
- This should be treated as a live application target if not already submitted.

### Passport Program
- Not applicable now.
- Keep only as future reference for transition to adulthood.

### ACSD
- Worth screening now if family income is within range and there are meaningful disability-related expenses.
- Official eligibility includes household income threshold and severity/cost review.
- Best use: help with recurring disability-related expenses and respite.
- Do not assume approval without income review.

### Respite funding
- There is no single universal "respite funding" stream for Alex right now.
- Most realistic current respite routes:
  - SSAH
  - ACSD
  - selected OAP foundational family supports
  - community nonprofit subsidies or local agency programs (still to be researched separately)
