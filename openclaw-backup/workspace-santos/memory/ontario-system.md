# Ontario Autism System

Last Updated: 2026-04-07

## OAP Overview

The Ontario Autism Program (OAP) is the main provincial autism program for children and youth under 18 in Ontario who have a written ASD diagnosis from a qualified professional.

For Alex, the important OAP pieces are:
- registration through AccessOAP
- foundational family services
- entry to school program when kindergarten approaches
- urgent response services if needs become urgent
- later access to core clinical services depending on ministry sequencing and capacity

### How OAP works in practice
1. Family registers through AccessOAP.
2. Family keeps account/contact info current and responds quickly to requests.
3. Registered families can access some supports before larger service movement, especially foundational family services.
4. Some services are universal to registered families, while more intensive clinical service access depends on program rules and capacity.
5. OAP communication is time-sensitive. Missed emails or forms can create delays.

## Current Wait Times

## Funding Tiers and Amounts

### OAP foundational family services
- No single standard cash amount.
- These are service supports available to registered families, such as caregiver coaching, transition help, and brief targeted services.

### OAP core clinical services
- Needs-based clinical service stream.
- Exact funding or service intensity is not a simple public age-band payment table in the current program description used here.
- Do not promise any specific dollar amount without an official current notice tied to Alex’s file.

### Legacy OAP childhood budgets
- Included here only because the family used this term.
- This is a pre-2021 registration stream and should not be assumed to apply to Alex.

### ACSD
- $25 to $665 per month, income and severity dependent.

### CDB
- Up to $3,411 per year, or $284.25 per month, for 2025-07 to 2026-06, if DTC-approved and income-qualified.

### Passport Program
- Not for Alex now.
- Adults 18+ only, minimum annual funding begins at $5,500 for eligible adults.

## Thames Valley District School Board (TVDSB) Autism Supports

TVDSB says students with ASD may be supported in several ways.

### Common school-based supports
- regular class with IEP
- in-class support
- in-school resource withdrawal
- speech and language support in the educational setting
- special education staff consultation
- ABA-informed strategies

### TVDSB teams relevant to Alex
- ABA Resource Team
- Speech and Language Pathology
- Special Education Resource Team
- START Team for short-term intensive support
- Psychological Services

### Higher-support placements if needed
TVDSB documents partial integration ASD classes through the IPRC process for students whose communication, sensory processing, and learning needs are too significant to be met in a regular class even with support.

For Alex, this is not a current decision. The first likely step is mainstream school planning with accommodations, then school review if needs are greater than expected.

### Parent documents TVDSB already publishes
- IEP guide
- IPRC guide
- communication guide
- program development team brochure
- ASD class program guide
