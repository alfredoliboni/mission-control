# Programs

Last Updated: 2026-04-07

## Gap Fillers

These are the most relevant community or publicly funded gap-fillers for Alex while the family waits for OAP-funded services.

### Preschool Speech and Language Program
- Fit for Alex: High. Alex has limited verbal language and is still waiting for SLP.
- How it helps: Publicly funded speech-language assessment and intervention from birth until school entry.
- London-area access: Thames Valley Preschool Speech and Language, delivered through Thames Valley Children’s Centre (TVCC).
- Contact: 519-663-0273 | 1-877-818-TALK (8255)
- Notes for family: No doctor referral is required. This is one of the best gap-fillers to keep active while waiting for private/funded SLP.

### Children’s Rehabilitation Services / TVCC
- Fit for Alex: High for OT, sensory regulation, fine motor support, and system navigation.
- How it helps: Children’s treatment centres provide rehabilitation services and may help connect families to related developmental supports.
- London-area hub: Thames Valley Children’s Centre, 779 Base Line Road East, London, ON N6C 5Y6, phone 519-685-8700.
- Notes for family: Alex already had an OT assessment with Sarah in 2025-03. Confirm whether Sarah is connected to TVCC or a private clinic.

### Foundational Family Services through OAP
- Fit for Alex: High.
- How it helps: Everyone registered in OAP can access foundational family services, including caregiver coaching, brief targeted supports, transition supports, and family resource events.
- Notes for family: This is often the fastest OAP service to use while waiting for larger funding or core clinical service entry.

### Entry to School Program
- Fit for Alex: Medium to High as he approaches kindergarten.
- How it helps: Supports children registered in OAP who are starting kindergarten or Grade 1.
- Notes for family: Important for transition planning, routines, communication supports, and school readiness.

### Community recreation / social participation supports
- Fit for Alex: Medium.
- Good targets: low-sensory swim, music, movement, or small-group play programs with parent participation.
- Why this matters: Alex likes water, music, blocks, animals, and responds well to structure. Community programs can support regulation and social practice even before formal therapy starts.
- Status: Specific London options still need to be shortlisted and verified.

### Respite and caregiver relief through SSAH or local supports
- Fit for family: High.
- How it helps: Parent relief, respite worker support, camps, before/after-school care, sensory items, and community participation expenses may be covered under SSAH if approved.
- Notes for family: This is the best non-OAP gap filler to pursue now if caregiving load is rising.

## Government Programs

### Ontario Autism Program (OAP)
- Relevance: Core autism program for Alex.
- Alex status: Registered in 2025-02 per family notes.
- Eligibility: Yes, because Alex is under 18, lives in Ontario, and has a written ASD diagnosis.
- Important detail: The family keeps referring to a "Childhood Budget," but official OAP childhood budgets are for children who registered before 2021-04. For Alex, this wording is likely being used informally and should be clarified with AccessOAP.
- Best next steps:
  - Confirm AccessOAP account is active and record transfer/consent is complete if needed.
  - Ask what current services Alex can access now: foundational family services, entry to school planning later, and any urgent response pathway if safety/behaviour needs become urgent.

### Passport Program
- Relevance: Not for Alex yet.
- Eligibility: No, not until adulthood. Passport is for Ontario adults age 18+ with developmental disability.
- Family note: Keep on long-range radar only. Not an action item now.

- Eligibility fit: Likely yes. Alex is under 18, lives at home, and has developmental disability-related support needs.
- What it may fund: respite, community participation support, sensory items, social skills programs, caregiver support, camps, some training, before/after-school or break care.
- Family-specific use cases:

- Status: Not yet documented as applied.

### Assistance for Children with Severe Disabilities (ACSD)
- Relevance: Worth screening now.
- Eligibility fit: Possibly yes, depending on household income and whether Alex’s disability-related costs and severity meet threshold.
- What it may help with: travel to appointments, special clothing/shoes, respite, some disability-related family costs.
- Income screen: Official page says total household income must be $76,920 or less, plus other eligibility factors.
- Status: Not yet documented as applied.
- Caveat: Do not assume approval. This depends on income, severity, and extraordinary disability-related costs.

## Educational Programs

### School-based special education supports
- Main route: school team, IEP, and if needed IPRC process.
- Relevance for Alex: Very important as he approaches kindergarten.
- Typical supports that may matter for Alex:
  - visual schedules
  - transition supports
  - sensory accommodations
  - speech/language support in school
  - ABA-informed classroom strategies
  - EA support only if school determines needed

### TVDSB autism-related supports
- TVDSB states students with ASD may receive support in:
  - regular class on an IEP
  - in-class assistance
  - in-school resource withdrawal
  - support from special education staff and speech/language services
  - partial integration ASD classes through IPRC when needs are significantly greater
- TVDSB also has:
  - ABA Resource Team
  - Speech and Language Pathology services
  - START Team for short-term intensive support
  - Special Education Resource Team

### Entry to School / transition planning
- As Alex gets closer to JK/SK entry, family should ask for:
  - transition meeting before school starts
  - written communication profile
  - sensory profile summary
  - safety/transition plan
  - first-month gradual transition plan if needed

### Key educational note for this family
- Alex’s strengths, visual learning style, sensory sensitivities, and need for predictable routines should be summarized early and shared with school.
- An IEP is not yet documented and likely does not exist yet if Alex has not started school.
