# Programs - Liam Okafor

Last Updated: 2026-04-07
Status: Working List

## Core Public Program
### Ontario Autism Program (OAP)
- Type: Provincial autism program
- Relevance: Main autism funding and service pathway for Liam
- Current family status: on waitlist for about 6 months
- Key streams to track:
  - core clinical services
  - foundational family services or other interim supports if available
  - caregiver-mediated and system navigation supports
- Dual diagnosis note: OAP is autism-focused, but Liam’s ADHD should still be documented because it affects service planning, therapy tolerance, and urgency.

## Ottawa-Area Gap Fillers
### Community-based recreation and adapted programs
- Purpose: lower-cost support while waiting for funded therapy
- Good target formats:
  - social skills groups
  - swim programs with staff comfortable with neurodivergent kids
  - Lego or interest-based groups
  - structured camps with low ratios
- Why useful for Liam:
  - supports regulation and confidence
  - can build social participation without waiting for full therapy access

### Parent coaching / brief consultation services
- Purpose: practical strategies while larger services are pending
- Good use cases:
  - home routines
  - transition support
  - school advocacy prep
  - managing combined rigidity, impulsivity, and emotional escalation

## ADHD-Specific Supports
### Medication management follow-up
- Type: medical support rather than therapy program
- Why it matters: may improve daily functioning enough to make school and therapy participation easier
- Best paired with:
  - teacher input
  - behavioural strategies
  - environmental accommodations

### ADHD parent education / coaching
- Potential benefit:
  - routines
  - emotional regulation support
  - homework and executive function strategies
- Dual diagnosis note: advice should be adapted for autism, especially around sensory needs and concrete communication.

## Summer Programs
### Ottawa summer camps for neurodivergent children
- Best-fit criteria:
  - trained staff
  - predictable routines
  - visual supports
  - quiet space / regulation supports
  - acceptance of movement needs and impulsivity
- Recommended search categories:
  - autism-friendly camps
  - adapted recreation camps
  - therapeutic camps
  - swimming-based summer programming
- Timing note: registrations may open before funding pathways move, so planning should happen early.

## After-School Programs
### Structured after-school supports
- Best-fit criteria:
  - short transitions
  - staff familiar with attention challenges
  - low-demand homework expectations unless specifically supported
  - clear pickup and communication routines for working parent schedules
- Good formats for Liam:
  - movement-based programs
  - small social groups
  - interest-led clubs with adult scaffolding

## Program Selection Rules for Liam
- Avoid programs that expect long seated compliance without regulation supports
- Prefer staff who understand the difference between dysregulation and defiance
- Ask whether children can access breaks, visuals, and movement tools
- Ask whether the program can handle both social communication support needs and impulsivity

## Immediate Program Priorities
1. OAP tracking and interim supports
2. Ottawa summer options with low ratios
3. After-school social or movement-based program
4. Parent coaching that addresses both ASD and ADHD
